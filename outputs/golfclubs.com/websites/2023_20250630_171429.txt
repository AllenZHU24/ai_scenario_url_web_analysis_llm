outingblog postschoosing a golf club shaft that is right for youwhen choosing a club shaft, it is important to note the shaft represents the engine of you golf swing generating both power and accuracy. the following are the factors which you should use to determine what is best for you:step 1choose between graphite and steel shafts. overall, steel shafts are heavier and are generally meant for stronger players. on the other hand, graphite shafts are lighter which allow you to increase swing speed.step 2pick shafts whose flex is right for your swing. simply, flex refers to the amount a shaft will bend while you swing. if you choose the wrong flex, the club head will not hit the ball squarely, which will have an impact on your distance and the trajectory of the golf ball.to accommodate most golfers, there are 4 different shafts types, ladies amateur, senior, regular, and stiff. if your drives carry less than 200 yards, you should probably have ladies amateur shafts, if your drive carries between 200 and 225 yards, you should use senior shafts, if your drive carries between 225 and 250 yards, you should use regular shafts, and long hitters who drive the ball 250+ yards should use stiff shafts.generally, if there is a doubt about which shafts you should have, choose the shafts with greater flex. shafts with ladies and senior flex tend to be whippier, creating a more left to right ball flight (a draw for right handed players), while stiffer shafts produce a more straight to right ball flight (a fade for right handed players).the following video provides
