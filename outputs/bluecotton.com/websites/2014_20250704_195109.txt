two recent college grads found themselves with a failed business venture and a small mountain of t-shirts. to pay for the faux pas they decided to turn the shirts into greek apparel and sell them to students from the local university. soon, production moved from a tiny apartment's living room to a more accommodating space, and the business grew past college campuses to include custom screen-printing and embroidery for all sorts of customers. a few more location moves and 20 years of technology later, bluecotton.com ships orders of all sizes to customers across the world. call it a
