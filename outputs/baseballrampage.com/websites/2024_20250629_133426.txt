the quality of your grip. this is important as you must keep a tight grip on the bat in order to hit the ball successfully. these gloves also provide ventilation to your hands. some batter gloves also come with cushions and padding which absorbs impact. this keeps your hands protected from the shock of a bat after you hit the ball. you must get gloves that are your size. also, keep in mind that the glove should complement your skill level and the grip of your bat. are there different sizes of gloves? for defending teams, gloves may be of different sizes. the sizes are relative to your position on the outfield. let’s go through these gloves for outfielders are long and have pockets as well as webbings infield gloves are small and have shallow pockets with tight webbings gloves for pitchers have closed webbing gloves for the first base don’t have fingers but have additional padding helmets helmets protect your head from any kind of dangerous pitching. these are available in different sizes for both adults and children. are there any regulations in place for helmets? batting helmets for the major league normally have an ear flap. this, as the name suggests, protects your ear from being hit by a ball. minor league players are required to wear a helmet that uses double ear flaps. catchers use a mask like a helmet. this helmet is also known as the “catcher’s mask” and has likeliness to the ice-hockey goaltender’s helmet. they have a mask that protects your face and part of the throat. another section of the helmet protects the top of your head. other protective gear padding for your ankles and legs is available. furthermore, you can also get padding for your arms. we found that these are designed to be lightweight as well as provide you with the maximum amount of
