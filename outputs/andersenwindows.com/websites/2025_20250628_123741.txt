tab)casement window faqs answeredwhat is a casement window?a casement window is a vertically oriented window with hinges along the side, so it opens like a door. the top-to-bottom opening allows in lots of fresh air. it’s one of the most popular types of windows because of its ventilating abilities and its versatile design. we offer styles ranging from traditional to contemporary along with a range of materials. find out everything you need to know about picking out a casement window. are casement windows more energy efficient? yes, casement windows are one of the most energy-efficient window types. with a single sash, they have a simplified design, which means fewer opportunities for leaking air. in addition, their seal is very secure because the hardware pulls the sash into the frame. it actually tightens when the wind blows. to make a casement window even more efficient, you can order it with triple-pane glass and a low-emissivity (low-e) coating through the e-series or a-series product lines. are push-out casement windows available? yes, we offer push-out casements through our e-series product line. sometimes called “french windows” these windows include two casements with hinges out the outside and no center stile (bar where the two windows meet), so they offer a completely clear opening. if you love their old-world charm, check out our e-series french casement window.what are the advantages of casement windows? where to begin? casement windows are versatile in terms of style — disguise them to look like a double-hung window or order them with a contemporary profile. they’re one of the most energy-efficient types of windows. their cranks make them easier to operate, especially if you’re reaching to open them. their verticality means they can let light and air absolutely pour into your space. want to dive into more details about the casement window? where do casement windows work best? while there’s no wrong answer to where casement windows work best, there are a couple of places where they truly shine. for example, in a kitchen where fresh air is important, you might be reaching over a counter or sink to open your window, and you might need a vertical window to fit between cabinets. casements are also a popular choice in more modern homes. their simple, single sash design means there’s more glass so the focus is on the view, rather than the window. but really, the range of options we offer means that a casement window can work anywhere. read more about the congenial casement window.how big are casement windows? casement windows are available in a wide range of sizes. go for the largest with our a-series casement window, which can be 4 feet wide and 8 feet tall. custom sizing is also available down to 1/8 inch. find standard and custom sizing details for all of our product lines in our sizing resources.what type of hardware do casement windows come with? casement windows can be ordered with hardware in a range of finishes and styles — from traditional to contemporary. you can even go for invisible hardware with the e-series casement window, which has the option to add powerassist™ power operation system, so you can open the window with the touch of a button. your exact options will depend on the product line you choose, but you can start exploring our window hardware.what do casement windows cost? the cost of a casement window, or any window, depends on a variety of factors. from the size of the window to the product line chosen to the options you add — the cost can vary. to start understanding what impacts a window’s cost, visit our pricing page. and if you’re ready for the kind of detailed information that will allow you to start budgeting for your project, we suggest you request a quote. what are casement windows made from? casement windows are available through all our product lines, so no matter the material you seek, we likely have a window that will work for your project. here’s a quick overview of the materials our casement windows are made from: a-series windows have wood interiors and a durable exterior made of fiberglass and fibrex® material. e-series windows are wood with a protective aluminum covering on the exterior. 400 series windows are wood with a low-maintenance vinyl covering on the exterior. 200 series windows are wood with a low-maintenance vinyl cladding on the exterior. 100 series windows are made completely of our innovative fibrex® material — the smart alternative to vinyl. do casement windows come with screens?
