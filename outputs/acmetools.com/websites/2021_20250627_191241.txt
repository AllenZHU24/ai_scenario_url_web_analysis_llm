trim grass to a uniform level. several models of lawn mowers are available, such as riding, self-propelled, walk-behind, and zero-turn mowers. the main debate is gas versus cordless, as well as push mowers. gas lawn mowers are the most common model type. they generate a lot of power, which helps them cut quickly, but it can take some effort to start pull-cord models. electric lawn mowers require no fuel, but you get tethered to an outlet, and the length of your extension cord limits your work area. they're ideal for homeowners who don't want to make a trip to the gas station in the middle of mowing. cordless lawn mowers combine the portability of gas-powered mowers with the easy-start ability of an electric model. make sure that you have a spare battery on the charger to swap out so that you can keep working when one battery's charge runs down. push lawn mowers run solely on your power. they're incredibly eco-friendly, but they do require more physical effort to push. push mowers are excellent options for neighborhoods with noise ordinances or for users who want a no-frills machine. they normally don't include a grass catcher, but optional attachments add this functionality. maintain your machine with lawn mower accessories. covers protect your mowers from the elements. special two-stroke oil mixes with gasoline to run gas-powered mowers, and they protect the motor against wear. extra blades cover you if you hit a rock or other obstruction and are essential for landscaping professionals. lawn mowers save you time and labor. they're easy to use and maintain so
