speak bra, a fashion staple that figures into your every day. need-to-know components come down to: the band, the cups, the straps and the center gore (the piece of fabric that connects the cups). anything beyond that comes down to personal preference. underwire vs. wire-free: some bras have u-shaped wires embedded below the cups for extra lift and support but these days, design advances in wire-free bra styles come pretty close—even for fuller cup sizes. unlined vs. padded: for an au natural feel, turn to multipart seamed or seamless mesh bras. if you prefer a little more coverage for modesty or fullness, you’ll want lightly lined or padded cups. front-close vs. back-close: choose a front-close style for the easiest possible on and off; consider a bra that fastens in back for added adjustability over time. back-smoothing vs. backless: is the way your back looks at the front of your mind when bra shopping? then you’ll want to home in on a back-smoother with a wide, shaping band, or a backless bra (no band, no bumps or lumps, no problem). straps: a bra's straps (which actually shouldn’t be doing much heavy lifting compared to the band) can be fixed in place, removable (voilà, instant strapless bra!) or convertible to turn them into, say, a racerback. fabrics: like other garments that are expected to really perform, keep an eye out for fabrics that offer a little something
