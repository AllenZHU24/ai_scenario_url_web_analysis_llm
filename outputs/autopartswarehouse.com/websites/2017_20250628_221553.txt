according to speed rating and tread design. there’s the standard or all-season tire, which comes with every brand-new vehicle. designed with grooves to channel away water, it’s meant for regular street driving during both wet and dry seasons. then there’s the performance tire used in racing. it’s softer and won’t do well on wet roads as a standard tire, but it can let you drive really fast on dry runways. lastly, there’s the winter tire, a specialized type designed to handle rain, snow, slush, and ice. now, aside from getting the right tire type to ensure proper traction in all kinds of
