it doesn't have to! by considering a few key factors, you will be able to narrow your options more easily and feel confident about your purchase after leaving the sales counter. 1. function who will use the furniture you're buying? knowing the answer to this question will help you determine the size, features and even what styles you should think about. do you have pets? kids? if you do, choose furniture made of durable woods and finishes. also look for patterned fabric that's washable, which can hide stains and pet hair a lot better. head here for more reasons why you should choose furniture by function over style. do you host often? consider tables with leaves that are easily extendable or sleeper sofas that can double as beds for weekend guests. also, think about furniture that doubles as storage, like and storage beds with drawers underneath. get more tips for maximizing a smaller space. 2. style when it comes to furniture, function is key but style is also important. opt for something that looks fresh, but not too trendy. the furniture you pick is a big commitment, so you don't want to get tired of your pieces too quickly. you should also choose pieces that are as neutral as possible. neutral pieces will still look fresh over a long period in multiple spaces. make sure you don't match the piece too closely to the style of a unique home, like an ornate victorian house, if you could be moving in just a few years. 3. comfort yes, you can count on the good, old-fashioned comfort test. how does your body feel when you sit on the furniture? this is a very important question when it comes to the mainstay pieces you will be using for long periods at a time such as sofas, recliners and mattresses. get more tips on picking the perfect mattress for you. and don't forget to test the comfort of table chairs. you want your table to be as inviting and cozy as possible when guests and family settle into their seats, even if it's a more formal dining set. shopping online? for your key pieces, do some online research and visit a showroom to get the real feel for the pieces you are considering and then make your final decision. 4. cost don't go furniture shopping without a budget in mind. knowing what you can and can't afford will make it easier to narrow down your options early. then, when you've really interacted with the furniture pieces you're thinking about buying, ask yourself if the price is right. also, check out any financing options that are available. this will help you to budget accordingly. before you buy, familiarize yourself with a store's return policies and their warranties. talk to a store associate if you have questions about warranties and returns. tuesday, december 20, 2011 | categories bedroom , before you buy , featured , function over form , furniture , mattress , shopping no comment yet post a comment older post home newer post subscribe to: post comments (atom) recent comments popular articles resizing for smaller spaces using scale a few factors to consider when buying furniture how to make a small room look bigger 5 furniture buying mistakes to avoid 5 things to ask a furniture sales associate blog archive ► 2012 (8) ► january (8) foam mattresses and spring mattresses: is there a ... 5 things to ask yourself before buying new furnitu... how to find your furniture style better sleep starts with a better mattress before you buy furniture, consider the size of you... how to rearrange furniture safely 5 things to ask a furniture sales associate 4 big benefits of latex beds ▼ 2011 (6) ▼ december (6) resizing for smaller spaces using scale do you really need a new mattress?
